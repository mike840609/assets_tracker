# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Assets Tracker serves individual investors and financially engaged power users who want one trustworthy place to monitor net worth, holdings, currencies, goals, FIRE projections, and historical performance. They use it in two modes: quick mobile check-ins that should feel native and calm, and denser desktop sessions for analysis, account maintenance, and reconciliation.

## Product Purpose

The product tracks assets, liabilities, holdings, transactions, snapshots, and multi-currency history so users can understand their financial position over time without losing fidelity when currencies or account structures change. Success means users can answer "where do I stand?", "what changed?", and "am I on track?" quickly, with enough depth to trust the numbers.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, capable, and precise. The interface should feel like a personal finance cockpit with native iOS manners: confident enough for market data, restrained enough for private money, and responsive enough that daily maintenance feels lightweight.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic fintech navy-and-gold dashboards, crypto-neon trading terminals, decorative SaaS glassmorphism, spreadsheet-like clutter on mobile, and web dialogs that break the installed-app illusion. Avoid hero-metric cliches when documenting future screens; numbers matter here, but the product should feel like a tool, not a pitch deck.

## Design Principles

1. Native where it matters: mobile surfaces should honor iOS patterns such as large titles, bottom navigation, safe areas, bottom sheets, swipe actions, haptics, and pull-to-refresh.
2. Trust through legibility: financial values, tables, charts, and history need stable alignment, clear hierarchy, and predictable state feedback.
3. Density is a setting, not a fight: desktop power users can choose compact layouts, while default mobile experiences stay comfortable and touch-safe.
4. Motion must explain state: use motion for navigation direction, theme reveal, haptic feedback, loading, and chart interaction, not decoration.
5. International by default: every user-facing pattern must survive English and Traditional Chinese copy, multiple currencies, privacy mode, and reduced motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain a WCAG AA baseline: visible focus rings, named icon controls, pressed/current state exposure, reduced-motion fallbacks, touch targets near native mobile expectations, and chart palettes that remain distinguishable across light and dark themes. Privacy mode is a first-class accessibility and situational-safety feature for using the app in public.
